Type 2 diabetes mellitus is characterized by the loss of cell function and mass, resulting from interactions between genetic predisposition and various environmental factors1 . One environmental condition identified as a risk factor for type 2 diabetes mellitus is circadian rhythm disruption, which is induced by shift work or sleep disturbance.
